Internet Stats (Middle East & Lebanon)

Introduction of the Internet to the Middle East

The middle east faced many obstacles in adopting the internet including infrastructure limitations, high costs and nearly every government censoring free and critical speech. Despite these challenges, the internet began spreading in the early 1900's countries like Tunisia and Iran and expanding to all countries in the region by 1999, except Iraq and Libya. [13]

In Lebanon, the internet arrived in 1995 but it was expensive and limited with a speed of only 14kbit/s. By 1998, just 2.7 users per 100 people were online. This number has grown exponentially, reaching 83.5 users per 100 people in 2023.

Summary

Lebanon's internet speed is below the global average of 44.67 Mbps for mobile internet. Looking ahead, initiatives such as improved mobile networks, fiber-optic expansion and Starlink could help Lebanon close the gap with regional leaders and improve internet services in social, educational and economic sectors.
